Dakshin Gangotri

Latitude: 70°4' S

Longitude: 12°0' E

ESTD: 1984

Description: Dakshin Gangotri was the first scientific base station of India situated in Antarctica, part of the Indian Antarctic Programme. It is located at a distance of 2,500 kilometres from the South Pole. It is currently being used as a supply base and transit camp. The base is named after Dakshin Gangotri Glacier.
